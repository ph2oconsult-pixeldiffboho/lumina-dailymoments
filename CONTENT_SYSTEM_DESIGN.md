# Content System Design: The Path (v1.0)

## 1. Core Topics (12 High-Frequency Experiences)

### Social & Belonging
1. **Friendship Conflict:** Disagreements or "mean" words from a friend.
2. **Comparison:** Feeling "less than" because of what someone else has or can do.
3. **Embarrassment:** The "wish I could disappear" feeling after a public slip-up.
4. **Being the "New" One:** Entering a group where everyone else already knows each other.

### Growth & Risk
5. **Making Mistakes:** The sting of getting something wrong or failing a task.
6. **Trying Something New:** The resistance to starting something unfamiliar or difficult.
7. **Independence:** The struggle of wanting to do it "myself" but hitting a limit.
8. **Performance Pressure:** The weight of being watched (sports, stage, school).

### Internal Regulation
9. **Anger / Frustration:** The "explosion" when things don't go as planned.
10. **Disappointment:** The "deflated" feeling when a hope is cancelled.
11. **Boredom:** The restless "itch" of having nothing to do.
12. **Overwhelm:** The "too much" feeling of a busy day or loud environment.

## 3. Journey Blueprint Template

- **Key Theme:** (e.g., "The sting of a mistake")
- **Emotional Arc Notes:**
    - *Start:* High intensity/shame.
    - *Middle:* Neutral observation (sitting with it).
    - *End:* Empowerment/Integration.
- **Real-Life Situations:** (3-4 concrete examples parents can relate to).
- **Tone Guidance:** (e.g., "Gentle, slightly playful, never condescending").
- **What to Avoid:** (e.g., "Avoid saying 'it's okay' or 'don't worry'—stay in the reality").

---

## 4. Differentiation Rules

1. **Metaphor Lockdown:** If "Friendship Conflict" uses "Spikes," no other journey can use "Spikes" as a primary metaphor.
2. **Physical Signature:** Each Day 4 "Release" must involve a different physical movement (e.g., Shaking vs. Jumping vs. Breathing vs. Tapping).
3. **Identity Specificity:** Day 7 statements must be unique. Avoid generic "I am brave." Use "I am an explorer of the unknown."
4. **Visual Palette:** Each journey should have a distinct "color mood" (e.g., Anger = Deep Red/Orange; Disappointment = Muted Blue/Gray).

---

## 5. Quality Checklist

- [ ] **Real Life Test:** Does this sound like a conversation at 7:00 PM, or a therapy session? (Aim for the former).
- [ ] **Text Density:** No more than 20 words per screen.
- [ ] **Child Agency:** Is the child *doing* the thinking, or is the app *telling* them what to think?
- [ ] **Parent Whisper:** Does the parent's prompt feel like a "secret weapon" or a "lecture"?
- [ ] **Day 4 Alignment:** Does the physical release actually match the energy of the emotion?
- [ ] **Day 7 Specificity:** Is the identity outcome something a child would actually feel proud of?

## 6. Top 5 Priority Topics (Next Build)

1. **Making Mistakes:** High frequency, high shame, clear differentiation from "Stuck."
2. **Friendship Conflict:** The #1 social struggle for school-age children.
3. **Anger / Frustration:** The most difficult emotion for parents to co-regulate.
4. **Disappointment:** A critical "resilience" builder for modern childhood.
5. **Trying Something New:** Essential for growth and overcoming "perfectionism" stalls.
